[MetaFactory](https://metafactory.ai/) needed auctions that respected **bid privacy** until the right moment, while still allowing verifiable settlement. Combining **PGP-style commitments** with on-chain phases turned that into a product feature—not a research paper.

## The use case

Fashion and culture drops attract bidders who will not tolerate leaky ascending auctions or opaque operator control. The system had to make **rules legible** and **reveal fair**.

## What we shipped

- **Cryptographic commitments** so bids stayed private until reveal.  
- **Stablecoin settlement** so treasuries and participants could reason in familiar units.  
- **UX that assumed non-crypto-native users**—clear states, failure modes, and recovery paths.

## Why sealed formats fit

Traditional open bidding **signals** too much mid-auction. Sealed designs reduce collusion and last-second gaming when paired with crisp timelines and auditable transitions.

This is Web3 doing what it is good at: **shared rules and settlement** with cryptography where the product genuinely needs it—not bolted on for headlines.
