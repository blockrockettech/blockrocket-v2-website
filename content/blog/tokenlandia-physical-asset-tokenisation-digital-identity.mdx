**TokenLandia** focused on **physical asset tokenisation**: connecting goods, documents, and identities to records that multiple parties could trust without a single opaque database.

## The use case

Physical assets need **continuity**: custody changes, inspections, encumbrances, and jurisdictions. A token is a handle; the **workflow and legal layer** decide whether that handle is useful in a dispute or a sale.

## Where we spent the time

- **Identity and binding** — how claims attach to assets and people without leaking more than necessary.  
- **Event history** — what gets anchored on-chain vs. what stays in private systems with selective disclosure.  
- **Interoperability** — standards and metadata so marketplaces and custodians do not each invent a silo.

## Takeaway

On-chain **provenance** is not “put it on the blockchain.” It is a negotiated answer to: **who is allowed to update what**, and **what happens when reality and the ledger disagree**?

Teams that answer those questions early ship calmer products. Teams that skip them ship tokens that lawyers cannot underwrite.
