In 2018, a guy from Texas turned up at **CoinFest Manchester**. He'd been thinking about art and NFTs the same way we had. His name was Erick Calderon — most of the ecosystem knows him now as [Snowfro](https://a16zcrypto.com/voices-onchain/erick-calderon/), the founder of Art Blocks. Back then he was just another curious person in the room, which is our favourite kind.

He came to one of our workshops on building NFTs. Then he came to the pop-up night that kickstarted KnownOrigin, and bought one of the first NFTs ever sold for crypto [NEEDS: which artwork / which artist?].

## Our second invoice ever

Erick wanted ideas built on-chain. At that point almost nobody offered smart contract development as a service — us, and a small outfit in Australia [NEEDS: name them?], and that was about it.

So we got to work: prototyping and early builds of art NFTs, called **Interface Token** at the time. It was the second piece of paid work BlockRocket ever did.

Andy still has his — [Interface Token #181273](https://opensea.io/item/ethereum/0x7f737b66921d18b008dc9c3a9b933a3bf559f1bc/181273), ranked #1 for rarity in the collection, sitting in the same wallet all these years later.

When the invoice went out, Erick made an offer: instead of ETH, he could pay with one of the CryptoPunks from his collection. A **zombie** variant. Roughly the same value either way — a few hundred dollars or so.

We were a brand-new agency with bills to pay. We took the ETH equivalent of the USD invoice.

## What zombie punks peaked at: $5M–$6.5M

There are only 88 zombie punks among the 10,000. At the top of the market, [zombie #7252 sold for 1,600 ETH (~$5.1M)](https://www.businessinsider.in/investment/news/nft-zombie-cryptopunk-7252-just-sold-for-5-billion-doubling-value-in-less-than-one-month/articleshow/85594193.cms) and [#8857 went for 2,000 ETH (~$6.5M)](https://www.larvalabs.com/cryptopunks/topsales). Ours-that-never-was cost a few hundred dollars to decline.

"Big mistake" is how Andy puts it now. But honestly:

> "In crypto these things happen and we was just happy to building cool things."

## The bit nobody expects

Here's the part that makes it more than a hard-luck story. Erick went on to build [Art Blocks](https://www.artblocks.io/) — and we built the first version of its smart contracts. The project went on to do hundreds of millions in volume [NEEDS: verify Art Blocks all-time volume figure + our contract's scope at publish time]. That first contract still tracks the famous **Chromie Squiggles**.

We passed on the zombie. We kept the work. The work turned out to be the thing that lasted — code we wrote in [NEEDS: year of Art Blocks v1 build — 2020?] is still doing its job on mainnet every day.

## What we tell clients now

Teams doing services-for-tokens deals ask us about this constantly, and the honest answer is the boring one: take payment you can run a company on, and if you want token upside, decide that deliberately with your treasury hat on — not invoice by invoice.
