# Andy Gray — voice reference

Source: medium.com/@awaygray and medium.com/blockrocket (2019–2020). Andy is the storyteller and community builder. Bio in his own words: "Builds stuff with Blockchain."

## Fingerprint

- **Conversational with asides.** Parentheticals carry the personality:
  > "It boils down to this nugget from the EIP ERC721 specification (don't try and read this paragraph while drunk…)"
  > "The MetaCartel! (can you spot us in the picture 👀)"
- **Admits the mess.** Friction is content, not something to hide:
  > "It's a bit of pain in all honesty"
  > "It felt hacky 😳, but, all the tests passed so we wrote some additional unit tests"
  > "But we had to do a bit of manual hand-cranking and pushing transactions around"
- **Four-word verdicts.** After the evidence, a flat punchline:
  > "It worked. 😏"
  > "Bit of a stalemate really 😐."
- **Stats as headings.** In data-led posts, the H2s ARE the numbers:
  > "## Total funds pledged: 881 DAI"
  > "## Realising a profit for share holders of > 6%"
- **Emoji as punctuation**, not decoration: 😐 👎 ✊ 😳 💚 😏 📈 🚀 🙏 — roughly one per paragraph, often closing a sentence.
- **British colloquialisms and puns:** "a fair few", "hand-cranking", "the dogs molochs" (a Moloch DAO dashboard named after "the dog's bollocks"), "trade like a boss".
- **Quotes real named people** with attribution, asks them to review for accuracy, and links generously — including to competitors.
- **Footer CTA pattern:**
  > "BlockRocket are based in Manchester and we help companies with blockchain and decentralised technology. Get in touch!"

## Grammar quirks to preserve (never fake new ones)

Comma splices ("It felt hacky 😳, but, all the tests passed"), occasional missing apostrophes, "BlockRocket are" (plural verb, very British). The texture is imperfect-but-clear.

## What Andy would never write

"We are a leading provider of innovative blockchain solutions." Anything with "journey" as a noun for a project. A paragraph without a single concrete noun.
