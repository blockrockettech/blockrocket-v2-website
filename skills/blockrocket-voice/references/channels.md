# Channel formats

## Website copy (blockrocket.tech)

Blended "we" voice. First person from the first sentence — visitors should meet Andy & James, not "BlockRocket, a consultancy". Frontmatter-driven (`content/pages/*.mdx` in the site repo): respect existing YAML keys, rewrite values only. Headings stay short and human ("This is not our first rodeo." is the house standard). CTAs are Andy-register: "Get in touch", not "Start a Conversation Today". Keep current SEO terms present somewhere in body copy: frontier technology consultancy, Web3, blockchain consultancy, Manchester.

## Blog posts (site `content/blog/*.mdx`)

- Frontmatter schema is documented in the repo CLAUDE.md (title, excerpt, date, thumbLabel, readTime, tags, coverImage…).
- Structure like their Medium work: hook with the concrete situation → what we did → what the numbers said → what still holds → human sign-off with links.
- Technical posts: include real endpoints, contract patterns, code where relevant (JS preferred). Andy-style stat headings work well.
- 600–1200 words. One image minimum if assets exist.
- End with contact line + links, never a moral.

## LinkedIn (Andy: linkedin.com/in/andrewmgray, James: linkedin.com/in/jamesemorgan)

- Individual voice, never brand voice — posts come from a person.
- 80–200 words. First line must work standalone (it's the fold): a concrete moment or number, not a thesis. "In 2018 we sold NFTs from a Manchester basement with QR codes." beats "Thrilled to share…".
- No "Thrilled/Honoured/Humbled to announce". No hashtag walls — 0–3 max, end of post.
- Paragraphs of 1–2 sentences. One idea per post.
- End with a real question or a plain invitation (Frontier Fridays is the evergreen CTA).
- James's LinkedIn leans analysis (a take + 3 supporting points); Andy's leans story (what happened Friday, who showed up, what was built).

## X / Twitter (@BlockRocketTech, James @jimbob_87)

- Single tweets > threads unless there's genuine narrative. If a thread, each tweet must stand alone.
- Andy register: punchy, emoji-closed, pun-friendly. "It worked. 😏" energy.
- James register: a hedged-but-firm take. "I would not bet against the casino though."
- Link in the final tweet, not every tweet. No engagement-bait ("What do YOU think? 👇").
- Brand account (@BlockRocketTech): announcements + reposts of founders, light Manchester pride.

## Cross-posting rule

One source piece (blog) → derive LinkedIn (the human angle) → derive X (the sharpest single line). Never paste the same text across channels; each gets rewritten in the channel's grain.
