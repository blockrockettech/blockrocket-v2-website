# 01 — Narrative Strategy

## The problem with the current copy

It's competent, but it reads like a consultancy describing itself. The phrases doing the heavy lifting — "battle-tested", "not innovation theatre, recycled decks, or second-hand conviction", "A track record, not a pitch deck" — are *claims about character* rather than *evidence of character*. The actual evidence (two engineers who hand-cranked a DAO in Osaka, wrote a royalty standard from a Manchester desk, ran a basement art show with QR codes) is buried or missing.

The fix is structural, not cosmetic: lead with the people and the story; let credibility be a consequence, not the headline.

## The story spine (one breath)

> Two engineers in Manchester got curious about Ethereum in 2017. They started a meetup to find other curious people. The meetup became a company, the company built KnownOrigin, eBay bought it. When that chapter closed, they came back to what they'd actually been doing all along: building innovative things with curious partners at the frontier — blockchain then, blockchain *and* AI now.

Every page, post, and social snippet should be a zoom-in on some part of that spine.

## The mission, stated plainly

**We build innovative things with curious partners in frontier tech.**

Unpacked:
- *Build* — we ship, we don't just advise (evidence: ethsync.wtf, stablemoney.dev, EIP-2981, KO itself)
- *Curious partners* — we pick clients the way we picked meetup mates: people genuinely interested in the problem (evidence: Frontier Fridays, Blockchain Manchester — they've literally been convening curious people for a decade)
- *Frontier tech* — blockchain is where we earned our stripes; AI and whatever's next is where the same curiosity points now

## Message architecture

| Layer | Message | Proof points |
|---|---|---|
| Emotional | "These are real people I'd enjoy working with" | Basement launch, pub meetups, Frontier Fridays, the WTF-is-an-NFT poster |
| Rational | "They've done the full cycle: build → scale → exit → return" | KO 2018→2022, eBay, EIP-2981 adoption, v2 client list |
| Action | "If you're curious about frontier tech, talk to them" | human@blockrocket.tech, Frontier Fridays invite |

## Handling the lows (decision: brief, not buried)

One honest line each, in sequence, no wallowing:
- NFT winter: "the people buying crypto-art could fit on a school bus" — already a great KO line, reuse it
- eBay ending: "eBay restructured in early 2024 and the KnownOrigin chapter closed. We took what we'd learned and came home to BlockRocket."

That's it. The brevity *is* the confidence.

## What "personality and growth" means in copy terms

- **2017 them:** curious, scrappy, slightly chaotic — testnet hacks, emoji-heavy blog posts, "It worked. 😏"
- **2026 them:** same curiosity, plus scar tissue — they now know what survives contact with regulators, Fortune-500 procurement, and bear markets
- The copy should *show both*: keep the warmth and the jokes, add the calm of people who've seen the cycle. Andy's old sign-off energy ("Get in touch!") beats "Start a Conversation Today".

## Anti-goals

- No invented anecdotes. Every story detail must come from the interview answers, docs/, or their published writing.
- No superlative stacking ("most respected", "globally recognised") without a source.
- No erasing blockchain to chase AI. Blockchain is the origin; AI is the continuation of the same curiosity.
