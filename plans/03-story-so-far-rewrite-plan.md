# 03 — Story So Far Rewrite Plan (`content/pages/story-so-far.mdx`)

The page structure (hero → chapter1 → timeline → pullquote → chapter2 → stats → cta) survives. The problem is register: it's an achievements page wearing a story costume. Every beat is a milestone; none is a memory.

## Reframe: from CV to campfire

Current chapters are organised by *what was achieved*. Reorganise the prose around *what it was like*:

### hero
- lead currently stacks five achievements in five sentences. Replace with one human sentence + one achievement sentence. The fragments style ("Ten years. One community.") is a known AI-tell — write it as actual sentences.

### chapter1 (2015–17, Community Roots)
- "It started in a basement, not a boardroom" — decent, but the basement was KO's launch (2018), so it's slightly mis-placed here. Chapter 1's true setting is **pubs and meetup rooms**.
- Rewrite around interview answers: the first meetup (venue, turnout, what went wrong), what they argued about, why they kept going.
- Keep the closer "No fund, no founders deck. Just a recurring calendar invite" — best line on the page, genuinely theirs in spirit.

### timeline
- Keep entries and dates (they're verified against docs/company-history.md) but rewrite `body` copy in first person. "Andy and James co-found…" → "We started a meetup because…". Third-person self-narration is the single biggest distance-creator on this page.
- 2022 entry: resolve the $7.8M / $40M discrepancy (see README).
- 2023–24 entry: apply the agreed one-line treatment of the layoffs/wind-down.

### pullquote
- The EIP-2981 "desk in Manchester" quote is strong. Trim "That's what battle-tested infrastructure looks like — quiet, durable, everywhere" → the triad + "battle-tested" is AI-cadence. Let the fact carry it: most NFT royalties still flow through a standard James co-wrote. Full stop.

### chapter2 (KnownOrigin)
- "A bedroom project that sparked a global movement" overclaims (it was a basement, and "global movement" is unverifiable). Try the school-bus line instead — KO's own retrospective humour is the right register.
- **Needs from interview:** launch-night story (Q5), first real sale (Q6), the eBay call (Q7).

### stats
- "A track record, not a pitch deck" heading is itself pitch-deck-speak. Replace with something flat and confident ("By the numbers" eyebrow already exists — the heading can simply be factual).
- Verify all four stat values before ship.

### cta
- "We've already seen — and built — what comes next." Logic wobble (you can't have already built what comes next). Replace with plain invitation in the agreed mission language: building innovative things with curious partners. End with Frontier Fridays as the open door — "come and find us on a Friday" is a better proof of approachability than any adjective.

## New optional block: "What we're like to work with"

If interview answers yield good material (Q9: a time they told a client *not* to use blockchain), consider one short section between chapter2 and stats. Honest advisory stories convert better for consultancy positioning than any service list. Requires a small template addition — only do it if the material earns it.
